Patient Risk Factors

Age:

The age of the patient in years. Examples: 33, 17, 35.

Gender:

Gender of the patient, represented numerically:

  • 1 for Male
  • 2 for Female
Example: 1 (Male), 2 (Female).

Air Pollution:

Level of exposure to air pollution. Higher values indicate higher exposure. Example: 2, 3, 4 represent increasing levels of air pollution (1 could be the lowest level, and 7 the highest).

Alcohol Use:

Frequency of alcohol use. Higher values likely represent more frequent consumption. Example: 4, 1, 7 represent varying levels of alcohol use (1 could be minimal use, and 7 could indicate excessive use).

Dust Allergy:

Degree of dust allergy. Higher values suggest more severe allergic reactions. Example: 5, 4, 6 indicate the severity of dust allergies.

Occupational Hazards:

Risk of cancer due to occupational exposure (e.g., working in harmful environments). Higher values represent greater risk. Example: 4, 3, 5 indicate varying degrees of risk.

Genetic Risk:

Level of genetic predisposition to cancer. Higher values mean a higher genetic risk. Example: 3, 4, 5 represent levels of genetic risk.

Chronic Lung Disease:

Whether the patient has chronic lung disease. Higher values likely represent more severe cases. Example: 2, 2, 7 could indicate the severity or presence of lung disease.

Balanced Diet:

Degree of adherence to a balanced diet. Higher values represent better dietary habits. Example: 4, 2, 6 reflect how balanced the patient's diet is.

Obesity:

Obesity level. Higher values likely represent more severe obesity. Example: 3, 2, 7 show varying levels of obesity.

Smoking:

Level of smoking. Higher values suggest more frequent or intense smoking habits. Example: 2, 4, 6 represent the severity of smoking behavior.

Passive Smoker:

Level of exposure to second-hand smoke. Higher values represent more exposure. Example: 2, 1, 7 show the level of passive smoking.

Chest Pain:

Presence of chest pain. Higher values could indicate more severe or frequent chest pain. Example: 2, 3, 4 represent the severity or frequency of chest pain.

Coughing of Blood:

Whether the patient has experienced coughing up blood. Higher values indicate a more frequent or severe occurrence. Example: 4, 2, 8 reflect the occurrence of this symptom.

Fatigue:

Level of fatigue. Higher values suggest more severe fatigue. Example: 3, 1, 7 show varying degrees of fatigue.

Weight Loss:

Whether the patient has experienced weight loss. Higher values indicate more severe weight loss. Example: 4, 3, 9 represent weight loss severity.

Shortness of Breath:

Severity of shortness of breath. Higher values indicate more severe difficulty breathing. Example: 2, 2, 2 could indicate levels of breathlessness.

Wheezing:

Presence of wheezing. Higher values indicate more frequent or severe wheezing. Example: 3, 4, 1 reflect the severity of wheezing.

Swallowing Difficulty:

Difficulty in swallowing. Higher values indicate more severe difficulty. Example: 1, 4, 3 represent the severity of swallowing issues.

Clubbing of Finger Nails:

Presence of finger nail clubbing, a possible sign of lung or heart disease. Higher values may indicate more severe cases. Example: 2, 2, 4 indicate varying degrees of nail clubbing.

Frequent Cold:

Frequency of cold-related symptoms. Higher values suggest more frequent occurrences. Example: 3, 2, 7 represent frequency.

Dry Cough:

Presence of dry cough. Higher values represent more severe or frequent occurrences. Example: 4, 1, 7 reflect dry cough severity.

Snoring:

Frequency or severity of snoring. Higher values likely represent more frequent or louder snoring. Example: 3, 2, 5 represent the intensity or frequency of snoring.

Level:

The predicted cancer risk level based on the input data. It is a classification, where:

  • Low
  • Medium
  • High
Example: Low, Medium, High represent different levels of cancer risk.
